# Vibe Coding

**Vibe Coding** describes the practice of writing software with AI coding assistants based on loose instructions, chat prompts, or "vibes," without a formal design or plan. The developer asks the AI to generate code, runs it, and then iteratively debugs it by asking the AI to fix errors as they arise.

While this approach feels fast initially, it is highly detrimental to long-term project health. Without explicit guidelines, the AI makes assumptions about conventions, leading to technical debt, inconsistent code, and a polluted context. In our standard, vibe coding is replaced by [Spec-Driven Development](Spec-Driven%20Development.md), where precise requirements are documented before implementation.
